## Even without the fuel type being included in the clustering there is a strong separation when tabling showing a clear pattern between the clustering variables and the fuel type

Cluster 1 – Low-Volume Natural Gas and Oil Plants -Below-average fuel received and energy production -Below-average total fuel cost -Low sulfur and ash content -Contains the majority of natural gas plants and all oil plants -Interpretation: Smaller or lower-utilization clean-burning facilities, including many peaking plants.

Cluster 2 – Large-Scale Natural Gas Plants -Highest fuel received units (+2.05) -Above-average total fuel received and fuel cost -Very low sulfur and ash content -Entirely natural gas -Interpretation: Large, high-output natural gas generating stations operating at a much larger scale than Cluster 1.

Cluster 3 – Fuel Cost Outlier -Extremely high fuel cost per MMBtu (+35.55) -All other variables below average -Only 4 observations -Interpretation: This cluster represents unusual fuel purchase records or pricing anomalies rather than a meaningful operational category. Because it contains only four observations, it should be interpreted as an outlier cluster rather than a typical group of power plants.

Cluster 4 – High-Output Coal Plants -Very high fuel energy content (+1.81) -Highest total energy received (+3.07) -High total fuel cost (+2.00) -Highest sulfur and ash content -Entirely coal -Interpretation: Large baseload coal plants with high fuel consumption and relatively high emissions characteristics.

Cluster 5 – Moderate Coal Plants -Above-average fuel energy content (+1.73) -Moderate fuel received and total fuel cost -Elevated sulfur and ash content -Entirely coal -Interpretation: Medium-sized coal facilities with similar fuel quality to Cluster 4 but operating at a smaller scale.
